Trish Clarke is a Whangārei-based sculptor working primarily in copper, steel and reclaimed materials. Her practice explores botanical form, environmental systems and the politics of place, with work ranging from large-scale public artworks to outdoor and gallery-based sculpture. She was one of three collaborating artists on the award-winning Camera Obscura (2020), a major civic artwork in Whangārei, and is an eight-time exhibiting artist at NZ Sculpture on the Shore. Her work has been presented in curated national sculpture exhibitions including Sculpture on the Gulf, Shapeshifter as part of the Wellington International Arts Festival, and Art in the Park at Eden Park, and she regularly exhibits in galleries across Aotearoa New Zealand.
